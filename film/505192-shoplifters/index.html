万引き家族

Shoplifters

2018 · 監督: 是枝裕和 · 120分 · ドラマ / 犯罪 / スリラー

東京の高層ビルの狭間に建つボロ家で暮らす一家。彼らに血のつながりはなく、わずかな収入と祖母の年金のほかは万引きで手に入れた物品で糊口をしのいでいた。やがて彼らのひそやかな生活に少しずつ崩壊の兆しが見え始める。疑似家族の姿をとおして現代社会の見えざる貧困を描き、第71回カンヌ国際映画祭パルム・ドールを獲得した。

Directed by Hirokazu Kore-eda of "The Third Murder" and "Our Little Sister," this human drama portrays human connections through the lens of a family that engages in petty crimes together. At the 71st Cannes Film Festival in 2018, it won the Palme d'Or, the top prize, for the first time for a Japanese film since "The Eel" in 1997. It also earned high praise overseas, including a nomination for Best Foreign Language Film at the 91st Academy Awards, the first for a Japanese film in a decade. At the 42nd Japan Academy Film Prize, it won eight awards, including Best Picture. In a working-class district of Tokyo, in an old one-story house seemingly left behind among high-rise apartments, live the homeowner Hatsue, the couple Osamu and Nobuyo, their son Shota, and Nobuyo's younger sister Aki, all dependent on Hatsue's pension. They make ends meet by shoplifting, living on the fringes of society, yet their days are filled with laughter. One winter day, Osamu brings home a little girl shivering on the balcony of a nearby apartment complex, and Nobuyo decides to raise her as a daughter. Triggered by an incident, the close-knit family begins to fall apart, revealing the secrets and desires each of them harbors. Alongside regular Kore-eda collaborators Lily Franky as the father Osamu, who repeatedly shoplifts with his son, and Kirin Kiki as Hatsue, the film marks the first Kore-eda works for Sakura Ando as Nobuyo and Mayu Matsuoka as Aki, Nobuyo's sister.
