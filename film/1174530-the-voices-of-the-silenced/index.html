よみがえる声

The Voices of the Silenced

2024 · 監督: 朴壽南 · 148分 · ドキュメンタリー

2025年に90歳を迎える在日朝鮮人2世の映画作家・朴壽南（パク・スナム）が、娘の朴麻衣と共同監督し、歴史に埋もれた声なき者たちの物語を描き出したドキュメンタリー。 1935年3月、三重県に生まれた在日朝鮮人2世の朴壽南は、1958年に起きた小松川事件の在日朝鮮人2世の少年死刑囚・李珍宇（イ・ジヌ）との往復書簡「罪と死と愛と」で注目を集め、その後も、植民地支配による強制連行や、広島と長崎で被爆した在日朝鮮人の声を掘り起こした証言集を出版。さらにペンをカメラに持ち替え、1986年に朝鮮人被爆者のドキュメンタリー映画「もうひとつのヒロシマ」で初監督を務めた。その後も「アリランのうた　オキナワからの証言」「ぬちがふぅ（命果報）　玉砕場からの証言」「沈黙　立ち上がる慰安婦」といったドキュメンタリー作品を送り出してきた。 本作は、そんな朴壽南がライフワークとしてきた朝鮮人原爆被爆者の実情と今日の課題に再び焦点を当て、約40年前から撮り続けていた16ミリフィルムを基に制作。広島や長崎で原爆被害を受けた朝鮮人のほか、長崎の軍艦島に連行された徴用工、沖縄戦の朝鮮人元軍属、そして日本軍の慰安婦にされた女性たちの声なき物語を描き出す。

A documentary co-directed by Park Soo-nam, a second-generation Zainichi Korean filmmaker who turns 90 in 2025, and her daughter Park Ma-i, depicting the stories of the voiceless buried in history. Park Soo-nam, a second-generation Zainichi Korean born in Mie Prefecture in March 1935, gained attention for her correspondence with Lee Jin-woo, a second-generation Zainichi Korean juvenile death row inmate in the 1958 Komatsugawa Incident, published as "Sin, Death, and Love." She subsequently published collections of testimonies uncovering the voices of Zainichi Koreans forcibly conscripted under colonial rule and those exposed to the atomic bombs in Hiroshima and Nagasaki. Switching from pen to camera, she made her directorial debut in 1986 with the documentary "Another Hiroshima" about Korean A-bomb victims. She has since produced documentary works such as "Song of Ariran: Testimony from Okinawa," "Nuchigafu (Life's Blessing): Testimony from the Place of Mass Death," and "Silence: The Rising Comfort Women." This film refocuses on the realities and contemporary challenges of Korean A-bomb victims, a lifework for Park Soo-nam, and is based on 16mm film she has been shooting for about 40 years. It depicts the voiceless stories of Koreans affected by the atomic bombs in Hiroshima and Nagasaki, forced laborers taken to Nagasaki's Battleship Island (Gunkanjima), former Korean civilian employees in the Battle of Okinawa, and women forced into Japanese military sexual slavery.
