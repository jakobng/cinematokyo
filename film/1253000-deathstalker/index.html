From Steven Kostanski, the mad genius director of "Psycho Goreman" (2020) and member of Canada's notorious effects collective Astron-6, who has contributed special effects to Hollywood blockbusters like "Resident Evil: Retribution" (2012) and "Pacific Rim" (2013). His two latest films, "Frankie Freako" and "Deathstalker," will be released sequentially in Japan starting July 3rd at Cinemart Shinjuku and other theaters nationwide. Kostanski, who wrote and directed both films, is known for his unique creature designs reminiscent of classics like "E.T." (1982), "Gremlins" (1984), and "The Goonies" (1985), blending 80s-style monster panic with absurdist black humor, creating a devoted fanbase. These new films bring his signature style to Japan. "Deathstalker" is a reboot of the 1984 cult classic "Deathstalker" (executive produced by Roger Corman). It reconstructs the sword-and-sorcery world with Kostanski's signature nightmarish horror and gore, infused with comedy and darkness. Daniel Bernhardt ("The Matrix Reloaded," "John Wick") stars as the titular Deathstalker. Slash, guitarist of Guns N' Roses, contributes to production and music. Synopsis: In a chaotic world ruled by sword and sorcery, the kingdom of Abraxion suffers under attacks from the sudden appearance of the crimson knight order, the Dread Knights, and is in decline. Unsettling rumors spread that this war is a precursor to the resurrection of the ancient sorcerer Necromemnon. Amidst this, the insolent wandering warrior Deathstalker comes into possession of a cursed amulet, making him a target for the Dread Knight's assassins. To break the curse and repel the approaching evil, Deathstalker embarks on a perilous journey, eventually being drawn into a battle that will decide the fate of the world.
