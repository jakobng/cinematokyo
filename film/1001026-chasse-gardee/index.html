デビッド・オズボーンの小説を原作に、「ミニミニ大作戦」などで知られるイギリスのピーター・コリンソン監督が、スペイン資本で1974年に手がけたサスペンススリラー。人間狩りを趣味とし、道徳観や倫理観を失った男たちの異常な姿を通して、人間と当時のアメリカ社会の闇を浮き彫りにした。 ベトナム帰還兵のケン、グレッグ、アートの3人はそれぞれ結婚し、子どもも生まれ、社会的成功者として絵に描いたような幸せを手に入れていた。しかし彼らには、「人間狩り」という誰にも話すことのできない趣味があった。ある時、弾丸と食料を買い込んだ3人は獲物を捜しながら狩猟場へと出発する。途中でカップルを誘拐し、目的地の無人島にやってきた3人は、カップルの女に料理を、男に給仕をさせ、何事もないように数日を過ごす。そして、ついに獲物であるカップルを解き放ち、狩猟を始めるが、島にはもうひとりのハンターがいた。 3人のハンターを「イージー★ライダー」のピーター・フォンダ、「バーバレラ」のジョン・フィリップ・ロー、「スケアクロウ」のリチャード・リンチが演じた。そのほか名優ウィリアム・ホールデンらが共演。日本では1975年の劇場公開と以後の数回のテレビ放送以外、ソフト化されることもなく、鑑賞が困難な幻の作品となっていたが、2026年1月に日本公開50周年を記念してリバイバル公開。

The madness of this nation has culminated in a human hunt. The long-lost, somber masterpiece once thought impossible to view is finally unleashed again. Directed by Peter Collinson (*The Italian Job*) Starring Peter Fonda (*Easy Rider*) Guest Star: William Holden <50th Anniversary of Japanese Release> *Open Season* (Japanese Title: *Dirty Hunter*) Confirmed for a nationwide theatrical rollout starting January 2, 2026 (Fri) at Cinemart Shinjuku and other theaters. Ken, Greg, and Art are three Vietnam veterans who have each married, had children, and enjoyed success in society, achieving a picture-perfect life of happiness. But they harbor a hobby they can tell no one about: human hunting. On the weekend the "season" opens, the three pack their bags and head for the hunting grounds in search of prey. Based on the novel *Open Season* by David Osborn, *Dirty Hunter* is a terrifying suspense-action film directed by the British maverick Peter Collinson—known for *The Italian Job* (1969)—and starring Peter Fonda of *Easy Rider* (1969) fame. Since its 1975 theatrical release in Japan and a few subsequent television broadcasts, the film was never released on home video and became a "phantom" movie nearly impossible to watch worldwide. Now, after half a century, it finally returns to the big screen. While it utilizes the "human hunting" premise found in many survival-action horror films, it eschews "Battle Royale" style entertainment. Instead, it highlights the darkness of America and humanity itself through the abnormal behavior of men who have completely lost their sense of ethics and morality—a vividness that remains undiminished today. The film possesses a grimness reminiscent of a fusion between Sam Peckinpah’s *Straw Dogs* (1971) and John Boorman’s *Deliverance* (1972). While it shares thematic similarities with films depicting the anguish of Vietnam veterans like *Soldier Blue* (1972), *Taxi Driver* (1976), and *Rolling Thunder* (1977), this Spanish production radiates a texture unlike any of them. From start to finish, the film offers no sense of catharsis, featuring a succession of events that betray any hope and long sequences of the hunters psychologically cornering their "prey." It is a masterpiece of traumatic cinema that wears down the viewer's nerves. Peter Fonda plays one of the human hunters; it is said he decided to take the role only after carefully rereading the original treatment multiple times. He is joined by John Phillip Law (*Barbarella*, 1967) and Richard Lynch (*Scarecrow*, 1973). Cornelia Sharpe (*Serpico*, 1973) and Alberto de Mendoza (*Horror Express*, 1972)—winner of the Argentine Film Critics Association Award—play the couple subjected to horrific psychological torment. Furthermore, Academy Award-winning legend William Holden appears as a guest star. The circumstances of why such a massive star appeared in this film remain unknown. In later years, Fonda remarked: "I really enjoyed that movie. It was the first time I played a real villain. I wish that movie would come out on VHS. I really love it." The newly released teaser trailer shows Fonda and the other two hunters capturing and tormenting the couple.
