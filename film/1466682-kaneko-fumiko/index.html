金子文子　何が私をこうさせたか

Kaneko Fumiko

2026 · 監督: 浜野佐知 · 121分 · 履歴 / ドラマ

約100年前に日本の国家権力に全力で抗った虚無主義者・無政府主義者の金子文子を主人公に、死刑判決から獄中での自死に至るまでの121日間を描いた伝記ドラマ。「雪子さんの足音」などの女性監督・浜野佐知が、金子文子の生の声を伝える短歌をもとに、彼女の孤独な闘いを描き出す。 1903年に生まれた金子文子は、父親が出生届を出さなかったため「無籍者」として育ち、9歳の時に朝鮮半島で暮らす祖母と叔母の家に引き取られた。植民地である朝鮮の村人たちを搾取する祖母一家から奴隷同然の虐待を受け、1919年には朝鮮の三・一独立運動を目撃。16歳で山梨の母の実家に戻され、その後東京で苦学した文子は、思想的にはキリスト教から社会主義、無政府主義を経て虚無主義にたどり着いた。やがて彼女は、朝鮮で独立運動に身を投じ日本に逃れて来た朴烈と出会う。同志にして恋人となった2人は、日本の帝国主義・植民地主義を批判する活動に奔走するが、1923年の関東大震災の際に検束され、死刑判決を受ける。その後、恩赦により無期懲役に減刑され栃木女子刑務所へ送られた文子は、たったひとりの獄中闘争を続けるが……。 菜葉菜が主演を務め、最後まで国家権力に反逆した文子の魂の叫びを体現。小林且弥が朴烈を演じ、三浦誠己、洞口依子、吉行和子が共演。

This biographical drama centers on Fumiko Kaneko, a nihilist and anarchist who fiercely resisted Japan's state power about 100 years ago, depicting the 121 days from her death sentence to her suicide in prison. Directed by female filmmaker Sachi Hamano (known for "Yukiko-san no Ashioto"), the film draws on tanka poems that convey Kaneko's own voice to portray her solitary struggle. Born in 1903, Kaneko grew up as a "non-registered person" because her father never filed her birth registration. At age nine, she was sent to live with her grandmother and aunt in Korea, where she suffered abuse akin to slavery from a family that exploited the colonized Korean villagers. In 1919, she witnessed the March First Independence Movement in Korea. Sent back to her mother's home in Yamanashi at 16, she later struggled through poverty in Tokyo, moving ideologically from Christianity to socialism, anarchism, and finally nihilism. She eventually met Park Yeol, a Korean independence activist who had fled to Japan. Becoming comrades and lovers, they campaigned against Japanese imperialism and colonialism, but were arrested during the 1923 Great Kanto Earthquake and sentenced to death. After her sentence was commuted to life imprisonment through amnesty, Kaneko was sent to Tochigi Women's Prison, where she continued her solitary fight behind bars. Nanana stars as Kaneko, embodying the soulful cry of a woman who defied state power to the end. Katsuya Kobayashi plays Park Yeol, with supporting performances by Masaki Miura, Yoko Horaguchi, and Kazuko Yoshiyuki.
