森に聴く Listen to the Forest

With forests covering 70% of its land, Japan is a nation defined by its woods. We embark on a journey to discover the true essence of the forests that surround us—from the foothills of Mt. Oakan in Hokkaido to Naruko in Miyagi, Hita in Oita, Mt. Ichifusa in Kumamoto, and Iriomote Island in Okinawa. Accompanied by leading Japanese researchers, this nature documentary ventures into ancient forests of giant trees that have survived far longer than humanity, exploring the mysteries of their intelligence. What is the world that these forests of multi-species coexistence, far beyond human imagination, are trying to tell us? Tetsujiro Yamagami, the producer behind acclaimed films such as *Village of Dreams*, *All Around Us*, and *The Lookout Generation*, reflects on his own life as he makes his feature-length directorial debut. He joins forces with Yuki Imai, who continues to create documentaries themed around the relationship between humanity and nature such as *Heguru*, to tackle this new subject. Within the eternal flow of time, how are nature and humans connected, and where are we headed next?
