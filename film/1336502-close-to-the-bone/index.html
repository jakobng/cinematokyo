骨を掘る男

Close to the Bone

2024 · 監督: 奥間勝也 · 115分 · ドキュメンタリー

沖縄戦の戦没者の遺骨を40年以上にわたって収集し続けてきた具志堅隆松さんを追ったドキュメンタリー。 沖縄本島には激戦地だった南部を中心に、住民の人々や旧日本軍兵士、さらには米軍兵士、朝鮮半島や台湾出身者たちの遺骨が、現在も3000柱近く眠っていると言われる。28歳から遺骨収集を続け、これまでに約400柱を探し出したという70歳の具志堅さんは、砕けて散乱した小さな骨や茶碗のひとかけら、手榴弾の破片、火炎放射の跡など、拾い集めた断片をもとに、その遺骨が兵士のものか民間人のものか、そしてどのような最期を遂げたのかを推察し、思いを馳せ、弔う。 自身も沖縄戦で大叔母を亡くした映画作家・奥間勝也監督が具志堅さんの遺骨収集に同行して大叔母の生きた痕跡を追い、沖縄戦のアーカイブ映像を交えながら、沖縄の歴史と現在を映し出す。

A documentary following Ryusho Gushiken, who has spent over 40 years collecting the remains of those who died in the Battle of Okinawa. It is said that nearly 3,000 sets of remains—of local residents, former Japanese soldiers, American soldiers, and people from the Korean Peninsula and Taiwan—still lie in southern Okinawa Island, the site of fierce fighting. Gushiken, now 70, began collecting remains at age 28 and has found approximately 400 sets. From fragments he gathers—crushed and scattered small bones, pieces of teacups, shrapnel, and traces of flamethrowers—he infers whether the remains are those of soldiers or civilians, how they met their end, and offers his thoughts and prayers. Film director Katsuya Okuma, who himself lost a great-aunt in the Battle of Okinawa, accompanies Gushiken on his collection efforts, searching for traces of his great-aunt's life. Interweaving archival footage of the Battle of Okinawa, the film illuminates Okinawa's history and present.
