太陽（ティダ）の運命

The Destiny of Tida: Okinawa’s Enduring Struggle

2025 · 監督: 佐古忠彦 · 129分 · ドキュメンタリー

「米軍が最も恐れた男　その名は、カメジロー」の佐古忠彦監督が、それぞれ国と激しく対峙した2人の沖縄県知事の姿を通して、沖縄現代史に切り込んだドキュメンタリー。 沖縄本土復帰後の第4代知事・大田昌秀（任期1990～98年）と第7代知事・翁長雄志（任期2014～18年）は、政治的立場は正反対でありながらも、ともに県民から幅広い支持を集め、保革にとらわれず県政を運営した。大田は1995年に軍用地強制使用の代理署名拒否、翁長は2015年に辺野古埋め立て承認の取り消しを巡って国と法廷で争い、民主主義や地方自治のあり方、そして国の矛盾を浮き彫りにした。 彼らの人生に関わった多くの人々の証言を交えながら、その人間的な魅力にも光を当て、それぞれの信念に生きた2人の不屈の闘いを描きだす。タイトルの「ティダ」は沖縄の方言で太陽の意味で、古くは首長＝リーダーを表した言葉。

Director Tadahiko Sako, known for "Kamejiro: The Man the U.S. Military Feared Most," delves into modern Okinawan history through the lives of two governors who fiercely confronted the national government. Masahide Ota (the 4th governor after Okinawa's reversion to Japan, serving 1990–98) and Takeshi Onaga (the 7th governor, serving 2014–18) held opposing political positions, yet both garnered broad support from the people and managed the prefecture by transcending the traditional conservative-liberal divide. Ota fought the state in court in 1995 over his refusal to sign for the forced use of military land, while Onaga did the same in 2015 regarding the revocation of the Henoko landfill approval; their battles brought to light the nature of democracy, local autonomy, and the contradictions of the nation. Incorporating testimonies from many who were involved in their lives, the film highlights their personal charisma and depicts the indomitable struggle of two men who lived by their convictions. The title "Tida" means "Sun" in the Okinawan dialect, a word that anciently referred to a chief or leader.
