A historical ensemble drama starring Haruma Miura, depicting the life of Tomoatsu Godai, a great man who built the foundations of modern Japan's economy and was called a rare 'Tengaramon' (a person with extraordinary talent). Written by Eriko Komatsu and directed by Mitsutoshi Tanaka, who previously collaborated on 'Ask This of Rikyu' and '1890', it is an original story. In the late Edo period, Japan is shocked by Perry's arrival. Young samurai Tomoatsu Godai (later known as Tomoatsu), sensing the dawn of a new era, ignores the internal strife over expelling foreigners versus opening the country and looks to the world. After meeting the courtesan Haru, he is driven by the desire to 'see a free dream' and joins forces with Ryoma Sakamoto, Yataro Iwasaki, Hirobumi Ito, and others to create a country where everyone can dream. Shohei Miura plays Godai's ally Ryoma Sakamoto, Takanori Nishikawa plays Yataro Iwasaki (who later founded the Mitsubishi conglomerate), Yuki Morinaga plays a young Hirobumi Ito (who became Japan's first Prime Minister), and Aoi Morikawa plays the courtesan Haru.
